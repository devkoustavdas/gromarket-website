Starting your **first job**? One of the first "financial" things you’ll be asked is to **open a salary account**. While it sounds simple, choosing the *right* salary account can save you fees, unlock premium banking features, and make your financial life smoother from Day 1.

This guide explains **how to open a salary account in India**, **documents required**, **features you must demand**, and answers common doubts like **ATM withdrawals, cheque book, and debit card charges**.

## What Is a Salary Account?

Banks and different financial institutions offer several types of accounts that individuals can choose according to their preferences. One such account type is a salary account.

A **salary account** is a special type of savings account opened by your employer in tie-up with a bank to credit your monthly salary.

**Key difference from a normal savings account:**

- **Zero minimum balance**
- Extra benefits like **free debit card**, **free cheque book**, and **higher transaction limits**

## Step-by-Step Process to Open a Salary Account in India

### Step 1: Check Your Employer’s Bank Tie-Up

Most companies already have partnerships with banks like:

* SBI
* HDFC Bank
* ICICI Bank
* Axis Bank
* Kotak Mahindra Bank

Your HR will usually share:

* Bank name
* Account opening link or form
* Relationship manager contact (sometimes)

**Tip:** Ask HR if you have flexibility to choose another bank if you already have an account.

### Step 2: Choose Online or Offline Account Opening

Most banks now offer **100% digital salary account opening**.

You can:

* Apply **online via bank website/app**
* Or visit the **branch** if digital KYC is not available

Online is faster and usually activated within **24–72 hours**.

### Step 3: Complete KYC (Know Your Customer)

You’ll need to submit documents (explained in detail below).
KYC can be:

* **Video KYC**
* **Aadhaar OTP-based eKYC**
* **In-person verification**

### Step 4: Account Activation & Salary Credit

Once verified:

* Account gets activated
* You receive **account number & IFSC**
* Share these details with HR/payroll
* Salary starts crediting from the next cycle

## Documents Required to Open a Salary Account

Most banks ask for **minimal documentation**:

### Mandatory Documents

* **PAN Card** (compulsory)
* **Aadhaar Card** (address proof & identity proof)

### Additional Documents (if asked)

* Employee offer letter or appointment letter
* Company ID card (sometimes)
* Passport-size photograph (offline cases)

**Good news:** For salaried employees, income proof is *not required*.

## Features Your Salary Account SHOULD Have (Non-Negotiable)

Before agreeing to any account, check these features:

### 1. Zero Balance Facility

You should **not** be required to maintain any minimum balance as long as salary is credited regularly.

### 2. Free Debit Card (Yes, You Get It Free)

- Most salary accounts provide a **free debit card**
- Usually Visa / Mastercard / RuPay
- Annual charges are **waived**

Some banks even offer:

* Contactless cards
* Higher ATM withdrawal limits
* Lounge access (for premium salary slabs)

### 3. Free ATM Withdrawals (Yes, You Can Withdraw Money)

You can:

* Withdraw cash from **any bank ATM**
* Get **5 free withdrawals per month** (RBI guideline)
* Unlimited free withdrawals from your own bank ATMs

After free limit, small charges apply (₹20–₹25 per transaction).

### 4. Free Cheque Book (Usually Included)

Yes, **salary accounts usually come with a free cheque book**.

* First cheque book: Free
* Additional cheque books: May be chargeable (bank-specific)

Even if you don’t use cheques often, having one helps for:

* Rent agreements
* Security deposits
* Official verifications

### 5. Net Banking & Mobile Banking Access

Your salary account should include:

* Free internet banking
* Mobile banking app
* UPI access (Google Pay, PhonePe, etc.)

### 6. Auto-Conversion to Savings Account (Important)

If salary stops crediting for **3–6 months**, banks may convert it to a regular savings account.

This may introduce:

* Minimum balance requirement
* Charges

Always confirm this rule upfront.

## Common Questions (Answered Clearly)

### Can I withdraw money from ATM using a salary account?

**Yes.** A salary account works like a normal savings account for ATM withdrawals, UPI payments, and transfers.

### Will I get a debit card free?

**Yes.** Salary accounts almost always provide a **free debit card** with no joining or annual fees.

### Will I get a cheque book free?

**Yes.** At least one cheque book is usually provided free of cost.

### Can I open a salary account without PAN?

**No.** PAN is mandatory for salary accounts in India.

### Can I keep two salary accounts?

Technically yes, but **only one can receive salary benefits** linked to your employer.

## Best Banks for Salary Accounts in India (Popular Choices)

* **HDFC Bank Salary Account** – Best digital experience
* **ICICI Bank Salary Account** – Fast online onboarding, accident insurance
* **Axis Bank Salary Account** – Good benefits for mid-income earners
* **SBI Salary Account** – Reliable, wide ATM network

## Final Advice for First-Time Job Holders

* Don’t blindly accept the default bank
* Ask about **charges after salary stops**
* Enable **SMS & email alerts**
* Update **nominee details immediately**
* Treat your salary account as the base of your financial life
